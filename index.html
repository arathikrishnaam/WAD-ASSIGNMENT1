About Kerala

Overview

Kerala, a coastal state in southwestern India, is renowned for its lush greenery, serene backwaters, and rich cultural heritage. Often referred to as "God's Own Country," Kerala offers a perfect blend of nature, history, and tradition.

Kerala's unique geographical features, such as the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea, contribute to its diverse climate and ecosystem. The state enjoys a tropical climate with monsoon rains enriching its lush landscapes.

Wildlife and Nature

Kerala is also home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and leopards. Visitors can explore the state's many national parks and wildlife sanctuaries to experience the beauty of nature up close. Notable sanctuaries include Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary and Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, which offer guided tours and trekking opportunities.

Kerala's rich biodiversity is supported by its varied landscapes, including dense forests, rolling hills, and sprawling grasslands. The state is a haven for bird watchers, with numerous species found in its protected areas.

Cultural Heritage

Kerala's cultural heritage is reflected in its traditional arts and crafts. The Kathakali dance, with its elaborate costumes and dramatic expressions, and Mohiniyattam dance, known for its graceful movements, are integral to the state's artistic tradition. Kerala mural paintings, which adorn temples and palaces, depict mythological stories and historical events with intricate detail.

These art forms are not just performances but an embodiment of the state's history and beliefs. Visitors can experience these cultural treasures at various festivals and events held throughout the year, such as the Thrissur Pooram and Onam festival.

Kerala Cuisine

Kerala's cuisine is a delightful blend of flavors, with coconut milk being a staple ingredient. Enjoy delicacies like Sadhya, a traditional vegetarian feast, and fresh seafood.

Kerala also has a rich tradition of Ayurvedic medicine, which uses herbs and spices to promote health and wellness. Visitors can experience Ayurvedic treatments at various resorts and spas throughout the state.
